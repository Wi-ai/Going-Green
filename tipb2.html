How to make ixora Leaf Paste/Tea and Decoction

Tea

Ingredients:

  • 1 tablespoon fresh Ixora flowers (washed)
  • 1 cup hot water
  • Honey or lemon (optional)
Steps:
  • Place the flowers in a cup.
  • Pour hot water over them.
  • Let steep for 5–7 minutes.
  • Strain and sip warm.
  • Add honey or lemon if you want extra flavor.
Note
  • Use max once a day, not long-term.

Ixora Leaf Paste (for minor wounds or skin irritation)

You’ll need:

  • Fresh Ixora leaves (washed)
  • A few drops of clean water
Steps:
  • Crush the leaves using a mortar & pestle or blend into a paste.
  • Apply directly to the affected area (small cuts, wounds, or skin irritation).
  • Leave for 10–15 minutes, then rinse off gently.
Note
  • Do a patch test first to check for skin reactions.

Ixora Flower Decoction (for diarrhea, as traditionally used)

Ingredients:

  • 1 tablespoon fresh or dried Ixora flowers
  • 1 cup water
How to prepare:
  • Boil the flowers in water for about 5–10 minutes.
  • Let it cool slightly and strain.
  • Sip slowly (traditionally taken once a day, but check with a herbalist or doctor first).

Notes:

  • This is not a replacement for medical treatment if you have serious symptoms.
Note for all
  • Use fresh, pesticide-free plants only.
  • Don’t overuse — herbal remedies are gentle but should be used mindfully.
  • If you have allergies, chronic illness, or are on medication, consult a professional first.

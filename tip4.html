Bitter Melon Tips

Learn more on Bitter Melon and how to boost healing

How to cook and make natural medicine/Tea

Cooking It

Methods Stir-Fried Bitter Melon method

    Ingredients:
  • Bitter melon
  • garlic
  • onion
  • egg or tofu
  • soy sauce
  • salt
  • Slice bitter melon thin, scoop out the seeds.
  • Soak slices in salt water for 10–15 minutes to reduce bitterness.
  • Stir-fry with garlic, onions, and eggs or tofu
  • Optional: add soy sauce, oyster sauce, or chili for extra flavor.
Bitter Melon Chips
  • Thinly slice, salt, and air-dry or bake until crispy.
  • Eat as a snack or crunchy topping.
Bitter Melon Juice (if you’re brave!)
    Ingredients:
  • 1 bitter melon
  • ½ lemon
  • 1 apple or cucumber (optional)
Steps:
  • Blend raw bitter melon with a bit of water.
  • Strain and drink 1–2 tablespoons (it’s super bitter, so mix with honey or lemon).
  • Juice is very bitter — start with small amounts!

Home Remedies with Bitter Melon

For Blood Sugar Control
  • Drink bitter melon juice (small amounts) — said to help lower blood sugar naturally.
  • Some people also boil bitter melon slices in water and drink the water as tea.
For Skin Problems (like acne or eczema)
  • Make a paste from bitter melon leaves or juice and apply it to the affected skin (do a small skin test first to avoid irritation).
For Digestion
  • Eating cooked bitter melon or drinking mild bitter melon tea can help stimulate digestion and ease constipation.
For Immunity Boost
  • Mix bitter melon juice with honey and lemon for a small daily tonic (helps fight infections, thanks to its antioxidant content).
For Liver Health
  • Mild bitter melon tea is believed in some traditions to support liver detoxification.
Note:
  • Always use in moderation; excessive intake can cause stomach upset or interact with diabetes medications.
TIP:
  • The bitterness can be mellowed by soaking slices in salt water, blanching them quickly, or pairing them with strong flavors like garlic, chili, or coconut.
Caution:
  • Pregnant women should avoid bitter melon juice, especially in large quantities.
  • Always consult a healthcare provider if using for serious conditions like diabetes.

How to make Hibiscus Tea

Tea

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons dried hibiscus petals (or ~4–5 fresh flowers, cleaned)
  • 2 cups water
  • Honey or sugar (optional)
  • Lemon or lime (optional, for tanginess)
  • Mint leaves (optional, for freshness)
Steps:
  • Boil the water.
  • Add dried petals (or fresh cleaned petals) to the hot water.
  • Let steep for 5–10 minutes — the water will turn a deep ruby red.
  • Strain the tea into a cup.
  • Sweeten with honey or sugar if desired, and add a splash of lemon or lime for extra flavor.
  • For iced hibiscus tea: Let it cool, pour over ice, and garnish with mint.
Note
  • Use max once a day, not long-term.

Hibiscus & Ginger Immunity Tea

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons dried hibiscus petals (or 4–5 fresh flowers, cleaned)
  • 1-inch piece fresh ginger, sliced
  • 2–3 cups water
  • 1–2 teaspoons honey or maple syrup (optional)
  • Juice of half a lemon (optional, for extra vitamin C)
Steps:
  • Bring water to a boil.
  • Add hibiscus petals and ginger slices.
  • Reduce heat and simmer for ~10 minutes.
  • Strain into a cup.
  • Add honey/maple syrup and lemon juice to taste.
Note
  • This tea is great warm on cool days or chilled with ice for a refreshing immunity boost!

Hibiscus Syrup (for Drinks, Desserts, Cocktails)

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup dried hibiscus petals
  • 2 cups water
  • 1 cup sugar (or adjust to taste)
  • Optional: 1 cinnamon stick, a few cloves, or a piece of orange peel for added flavor
Steps:
  • Combine water, hibiscus, and sugar in a pot (plus optional spices).
  • Bring to a boil, stir until sugar dissolves.
  • Reduce heat and simmer for 10–15 minutes.
  • Strain the liquid, discard petals and spices.
  • Let the syrup cool and store in a glass bottle/jar in the fridge.

Use it in

  • Sparkling water
  • lemonade
  • over pancakes
  • as a cocktail/mocktail mixer
Note for all
  • Use fresh, pesticide-free plants only.
  • Don’t overuse — herbal remedies are gentle but should be used mindfully.
  • If you have allergies, chronic illness, or are on medication, consult a professional first.

Hibiscus Hair Rinse

Ingredients:

  • 2 tablespoons dried hibiscus petals (or 4–5 fresh flowers)
  • 2 cups water
  • Optional: 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar or a few drops of lavender oil
steps:
  • Boil water and pour over hibiscus petals.
  • Let steep until cool (about 30–45 minutes).
  • Strain out petals.
  • After shampooing, pour the hibiscus rinse over your hair as a final rinse — leave it in or rinse lightly with cool water.

Hibiscus Skin Toner

Ingredients:

  • 1 tablespoon dried hibiscus petals
  • 1/2 cup boiling water
  • Optional: 1 tablespoon rose water or aloe vera juice
steps:
  • Steep hibiscus petals in boiling water for 10 minutes.
  • Strain and let cool.
  • Mix in rose water or aloe vera if using.
  • Store in a small spray bottle or jar in the fridge for up to a week.
  • Use as a facial mist or apply with a cotton pad after cleansing.

Hibiscus-Infused Vinegar (for salads, marinades, hair rinse, or skin toner)

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup dried hibiscus petals
  • 2 cups apple cider vinegar or white vinegar
  • Optional: add herbs like rosemary, thyme, or citrus peel
steps:
  • Place hibiscus petals in a clean glass jar.
  • Pour vinegar over the petals.
  • Seal the jar tightly and let steep in a cool, dark place for 1–2 weeks.
  • Strain and store in a glass bottle.
Uses:
  • Add to salads or marinades for a tangy floral twist.
  • Use as a diluted hair rinse (1 part vinegar, 3 parts water) for shine.
  • Dilute with water and use as a skin toner (test on a small patch first).

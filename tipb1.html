How to make Giloy tincture/Tea

Tea

Ingredients:

  • 1–2 small Giloy stems (about 6–8 inches) OR 1 tsp Giloy powder
  • 1–2 cups water
  • Optional: a few tulsi (holy basil) leaves, a small piece of ginger, or a pinch of black pepper for added health benefits
Steps:
  • Boil the water.
  • Add the tulsi leaves to a teapot or cup.
  • Pour boiling water over the leaves.
  • Cover and let steep for 5–10 minutes.
  • Strain the tea into a cup.
  • Add honey, lemon, or ginger if you like.
  • Sip slowly and enjoy!

Giloy Juice

You’ll need:

  • Fresh or dried Giloy stems (chopped)
  • 40–50% alcohol (vodka or rum works well)
  • A clean glass jar with a lid
Steps:
  • Fill the jar halfway with chopped Giloy.
  • Pour alcohol over it until the jar is full, covering all the herb.
  • Seal the jar tightly and shake it gently.
  • Store in a cool, dark place for 4–6 weeks, shaking the jar every few days.
  • After the infusion period, strain the liquid through cheesecloth or a fine sieve.
  • Store the tincture in a dark glass bottle with a dropper.
  • Drink warm — once daily is enough for general immunity boosting.
Dosage:
  • Usually 10–20 drops diluted in water, once or twice daily.

Important Notes:

  • Always consult a doctor before long-term use, especially if you have autoimmune issues, are pregnant, or take medications.

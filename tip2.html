How to make natural medicine/Tea

Tea

Ingredients:

  • 1–2 teaspoons dried tulsi leaves (or a handful of fresh leaves)
  • 1 cup (250 ml) boiling water
  • Optional: honey, lemon, ginger.
Steps:
  • Boil the water.
  • Add the tulsi leaves to a teapot or cup.
  • Pour boiling water over the leaves.
  • Cover and let steep for 5–10 minutes.
  • Strain the tea into a cup.
  • Add honey, lemon, or ginger if you like.
  • Sip slowly and enjoy!

Holy Basil Tincture

You’ll need:

  • Fresh or dried tulsi leaves (about 1 cup fresh or ½ cup dried)
  • High-proof alcohol (like vodka, 40–60% alcohol),
  • A clean glass jar with a tight lid,
  • Cheesecloth or fine strainer,
  • A small dark glass dropper bottle (for storage)
Steps:
  • Chop or lightly crush the tulsi leaves to release their oils.
  • Place the leaves in the clean jar, filling it halfway.
  • Pour alcohol over the leaves until they’re completely covered (about 1–1.5 inches above the herbs).
  • Seal the jar tightly.
  • Store in a cool, dark place for 4–6 weeks, shaking it gently every few days.
  • After 4–6 weeks, strain the mixture through cheesecloth into a clean bowl.
  • Pour the strained liquid (your tincture!) into a dark glass dropper bottle.
How to Use:
  • Typical dose is 1–2 dropperfuls (about 30–60 drops) diluted in water or tea, 1–3 times per day.
  • Always start with small doses to check your body’s reaction.

Important Notes:

  • Avoid tinctures if you’re sensitive to alcohol. You can use glycerin instead, but the shelf life will be shorter.
  • If you’re pregnant, nursing, or taking medications, check with a healthcare provider before using tulsi in large amounts or concentrated forms.
